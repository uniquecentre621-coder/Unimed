Privacy Policy

How Unique Medical Centre collects, uses, and protects your personal and health information.

Practice: Unique Medical Centre

Practitioner: Dr O.K Idugboe (PR 1185918)

Last Updated: 2025

Governing Law: Republic of South Africa (POPIA)

1 Introduction

Unique Medical Centre ("we", "our", or "the practice"), operated by Dr O.K Idugboe (Practice Number PR 1185918), is committed to protecting your privacy and the confidentiality of your personal and health information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your information in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA) and all applicable South African healthcare legislation.

By visiting our practice or using our website, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

2 Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal information:

  • Identity Information: Full name, date of birth, gender, South African ID number or passport number.
  • Contact Information: Home address, phone number, email address, and WhatsApp contact details.
  • Health Information: Medical history, diagnoses, medications, test results, treatment plans, and other clinical information gathered during consultations.
  • Medical Aid Information: Medical aid scheme name, membership number, and principal member details.
  • Financial Information: Billing and payment information for consultation fees (where applicable).
  • Next of Kin: Emergency contact information where provided.

3 How We Use Your Information

We use your personal and health information for the following purposes:

  • To provide medical consultations, diagnosis, treatment, and follow-up care.
  • To compile and maintain clinical records as required by law and professional standards.
  • To process medical aid claims and billing.
  • To issue medical certificates, sick notes, referral letters, and other clinical documents.
  • To conduct SASSA medical assessments and complete the required documentation.
  • To communicate with you regarding your appointments and healthcare.
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations, including HPCSA requirements.
  • To refer you to specialists or hospitals when clinically indicated.

4 Confidentiality of Health Information

Your health information is strictly confidential. We will not disclose your medical information to any third party without your explicit consent, except in the following circumstances permitted or required by law:

  • Where disclosure is required by a court order or other legal obligation.
  • Where disclosure is necessary to prevent serious harm to yourself or others.
  • Where disclosure is required for public health purposes under the National Health Act.
  • Where information is shared with another healthcare provider involved in your direct care (with your knowledge).
  • Where information is submitted to your medical aid scheme for billing and claims purposes.

All staff members of Unique Medical Centre are bound by professional and contractual obligations of confidentiality.

5 Storage and Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information from unauthorised access, loss, or disclosure. Patient records are stored securely and access is restricted to authorised personnel only.

Clinical records are retained for a minimum of six (6) years as required under South African healthcare law. Records of minor patients are retained until the patient reaches adulthood, plus six years thereafter.

6 Your Rights (POPIA)

Under the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA), you have the following rights:

  • Right to Access: You may request access to the personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to Correction: You may request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to Deletion: You may request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Right to Object: You may object to the processing of your personal information in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Complain: If you believe your rights have been violated, you may lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator of South Africa.

7 Website and Digital Communication

Our website does not collect personal information without your knowledge. Any information you submit via our contact channels (phone, WhatsApp, email) will be used solely to respond to your enquiry and schedule appointments.

We may use anonymous analytics tools to understand how visitors use our website. No personally identifiable information is collected through these tools.

Please be aware that communication via WhatsApp and email carries inherent security limitations. For sensitive clinical matters, we recommend discussing these in person during a consultation.

8 Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. The updated policy will be posted on our website. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
